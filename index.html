Dual-Mode System

Two states, one brand

The color system signals intent. White surface and blue accent means you're in the Lab — building tools, shipping systems, writing documentation. Warm paper and orange accent means you're in the Studio — writing strategy, designing campaigns, presenting to clients.

Same person, different room. Both modes share the same authentic voice — contractions mandatory, no AI slop, the collar stays loose. The surface changes; the person doesn't.

Math-based geometry. viewBox 0 0 500 440. Renders in --ink by default, --accent for emphasis. One mark, both modes.

Voice & Tone

How the brand speaks

Same person, different room. Both modes share the authentic voice — contractions mandatory, no AI slop, the collar stays loose.

Lab Voice Technical, direct
"This pipeline watches the inbox, transcribes audio through Whisper, and writes polished notes to the vault. It runs on login."
Precise. Confident. Says what it does, not what it could do.
Studio Voice Editorial, strategic
"The homepage isn't a feature list — it's a first impression. We rewrote it as a story about what you actually do for people."
Warm. Opinionated. Earns trust before it asks for anything.
Principles
  • 01

    Contractions are mandatory. "It's" not "It is." "You're" not "You are." Stiff language signals a brand that doesn't trust itself.

  • 02

    No AI slop. "Leverage," "synergize," "unlock potential" — these are words that mean nothing and say less. Every sentence should be able to defend itself.

  • 03

    The collar stays loose. Expertise without arrogance. Confident without being a prick about it. The goal is to make the reader feel smarter, not smaller.

  • 04

    Specificity over generality. "12 metro locations" beats "multiple locations." "Whisper" beats "an AI transcription tool." Details are credibility.

  • 05

    Curiosity as a discipline. Every piece of writing should make the reader want to know what comes next. If it doesn't, cut it.
