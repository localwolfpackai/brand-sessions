01 — Brand identity

Craft-obsessed solo builder. Senior creative director energy.

Lupo Studios is the creative umbrella of Anthony Lupo. Not an agency. Not a SaaS company. A solo builder who uses AI to close the gap between ideas and execution.

Ten years in tech sales at Salesforce, Intuit, Samsara, Yext. That's the corporate version. The other version started in 2023 — teaching himself AI, building at the CLI at midnight, breaking and rebuilding workflows until they worked.

No CS degree. No bootcamp. Just consistent curiosity applied over time. Custom Claude skills, MCP server integrations, brand systems built through conversation, AI agent workflows across development, design, sales, and marketing.

The baseline is AI. The differentiator is taste. Velocity comes from the tools. Soul comes from the person.

The edge is not using AI — everyone can now. It's having logged enough reps to know what works, what's a dead end, and what to try first. The difference between someone who adopted a tool and someone you'd actually call.

A quote from a Stanford professor father-in-law: always question your assumptions. You don't need a technical background to be the person people trust on AI strategy. You need the willingness to learn, experiment, and fail softly.

04 — Typography

Three families. Two per page. Type as a design element.

Instrument Serif for editorial headlines. DM Sans for comfortable body text. Geist Mono for system labels and code. Never more than two families plus mono on a single page.

The work's all here.

Letter-spacing Tighten headings -0.03em. Loosen body 1.6 line-height. These are design tools, not defaults.
05 — Components & motion

Handcrafted, not templated. Editorial, not dashboard.

This is a solo creative studio. Every project should feel intentional. shadcn/ui and Radix are functional starting points, not design systems. Always restyle to match the project's personality.

  • Grid is the anchor 8pt spacing scale and 12-column layout for the skeleton. Container logic stays strict to prevent breakage. Components are the experimental sandbox inside grid cells.
  • Vary the pattern Never use the same card style twice on one page. Mix full-bleed sections with contained content. Use asymmetric column spans (3/9, 4/8, 5/7) instead of defaulting to equal splits.
  • Type as design element Oversized headings, mixed weights, creative line breaks. Hero moments use multiple expressive tactics to break from predictable patterns.
  • Motion with purpose Scroll-triggered reveals, staggered entrances, parallax. Hover means shape change, not just color swap. Elements should arrive, not appear.
  • Glass panel as default surface rgba(255, 253, 249, 0.6) + backdrop-filter: blur(20px) + subtle border. Depth from elevation, not noise or grain.
  • Borders tell the story Active = stronger border. Resting = barely visible. Hover = intermediate. Vary opacity and color for state, not just background changes.

What to avoid

  • Cookie-cutter SaaS templates and "corporate clean" aesthetic
  • Identical card grids repeated across sections
  • Generic hero + 3-feature-cards + pricing table layout
  • Unmodified shadcn/ui defaults without visual customization
  • Neon glows, circuit lines, dark-mode hacker cosplay, purple accents
  • Using ease or ease-in-out as default timing functions
  • Noise textures or feTurbulence overlays on full surfaces

Physics easing

Expo out cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) Most common. Snappy feel. Elements entering view, reveals, hover.
Back out cubic-bezier(0.175, 0.885, 0.32, 1.275) Slight overshoot. Playful entries, bouncy interactions.
Quint in-out cubic-bezier(0.83, 0, 0.17, 1) Smooth symmetrical. State changes, toggles, theme transitions.
06 — Voice & tone

Would a real person say this to a friend over a beer?

If not, rewrite it. The one-line test for every piece of copy. Senior Creative Director energy, coffee-shop delivery. Never performing expertise. Just showing the work.

Warm but not soft. Confident but not loud. Specific but not technical for its own sake. Funny when it lands. Silent when it doesn't.

Share the process, not just the advice. Adjust the tone for the audience. For non-technical folks: metaphors, side-by-sides, plain English. For those with tech knowledge: what worked, how it's structured, how to repeat it. Be honest about your level. You're not a backend dev. You're someone who figured out it wasn't that hard — and can show exactly how.

Observations over opinions. Show the thing. If you have to explain why it's interesting, cut the line. Specificity is confidence. "I build things" is weak. "The commit message is the liner note" earns its place.

Curiosity as Discipline
Technical barriers are curiosity barriers. Lead with what you're learning, not what you've mastered. Experimentation is a daily practice, not a personality trait.
The Radius
No niche. A radius. AI Strategy, Design, Art, Culture — the intersection is where the work gets interesting. Connecting disparate things is the method.
The Human Baseline
AI is the baseline. The differentiator is curation. The 1% of output that actually looks, feels, and functions like high-end human design. AI provides velocity. You provide taste and direction.

Tone spectrum

Warm Confident Specific Relaxed Direct Curious

Sentence-level rules

  • Short sentences hit harder. Use them on purpose.
  • Contractions mandatory. You're, we'll, it's.
  • If it can be cut in half, cut it.
  • No jargon without a plain-English translation nearby.
  • Every line earns its place or it doesn't stay.
  • Metaphors are load-bearing. They're how ideas travel.

Banned words

  • revolutionary
  • game-changing
  • unlock potential
  • delve
  • forge / nexus
  • synergy / seamless
  • landscape
  • leverage (say "using")
  • robust / pivotal
  • deep dive / journey

Banned aesthetics

  • purple gradients
  • generic sans-serif on black
  • "future" as a visual direction
  • neon glows / circuit lines
07 — Workspace

Numbered lanes, kebab-case, one level deep.

Projects are organized in numbered lanes using Johnny Decimal conventions. Files are kebab-case. Directories sort-first in every tool. If you don't know where something goes, it lands in 00-inbox.
